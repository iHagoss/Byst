Backgauges

This tab is used to create the backgauges for all bending processes. Backgauges are needed for precise positioning of the bending part prior to making a bend. The backgauges can take the form of a right and left backgauge finger minimum.

Below the view window are tables displaying the defined entries and the status of the backgauges for each of the bending processes.

Tip: Tap on the appropriate backgauge in the table to call up the dialog to edit backgauges.

Positioning backgauges using the pedal

If the backgauges will be moved forwards towards the operator for the next bending process, the operator must press the pedal to confirm the repositioning of the backgauges when the new position lies more than a certain distance away. The backgauges will then move to the relevant position.

Confirmation safeguards the operator by preventing the bending aid from thrusting towards the operator during automatic positioning of bending parts with long sides in the rear machine area.

NOTE: The minimum distance at and above which confirmation using the pedal is required is defined in the bending database.

What each column means:

Status

Displays the status of the backgauges in the bending sequence. In each case this is the status for the current bend.

Symbol Description
A valid backgauge is assigned to the bending process.
A valid backgauge has not yet been assigned to the bending process.
The backgauge for the bending process has not been checked yet. Run the Check function.

Angle

Shows the angle of the bend. An angle of 0 is only used for the final bend in a hemming operation.

Bend The bend is an element of the profile programming and designates a change in shape of the workpiece. A number of attributes can be assigned to a bend: side length, bend angle, bend radius (optional), initial-bend angle (optional), additional attributes (optional). A bend can consist of several bending processes. type

Shows the type of bend in the bending process.

  • Normal bending Normal bending is the standard method for creating a bend. Bending is defined by the bend angle and an optional initial-bend angle.
  • Hemming This bend type bends the sheet over flat with a bend angle of 180°. The bending action is completed in two consecutive steps of «Pre-bend» and Hem. These steps may also be performed separately from one another.
  • Pre-bending This bend type creates a bend in two steps. The bend is first made with an initial-bend angle and then the finished bend is made later with a final angle. Use pre-bending for complex bending parts to avoid collisions during the bending process.
  • Auxiliary bend The auxiliary bend is temporarily inserted for certain bend shapes, and then bent back again, to allow the final shape of the bending part to be achieved.
  • Stepped radius A stepped radius bend is made when the angle and radius of a bent side is achieved by making a number of individual bends. bend

Backgauge

Shows the number of the backgauge finger. The active backgauge finger is shown in blue in the 3D view.

Parked

Shows whether the backgauge finger is parked or positioned.

Snap

Shows whether the corners of the bending part are snapped. Snapping refers to positioning two points in the backgauge finger for corner stops (See «Snap»).

Symbol Description
Snapping is enabled. The backgauge snaps correctly to a corner of the part.
Snapping is disabled. The backgauge is situated on a straight edge of the part.
Snapping is disabled. The backgauge is situated on an angled edge of the part.
The symbol indicates the backgauge as a master backgauge. The master backgauge determines the position of the R axis for all backgauges.

NOTE: The master backgauge is only used for backgauges with a common R axis.

Reference point

Shows the position on the bending-part contact edge where the backgauge finger provides support.

Retraction Retraction refers to the backwards movement of the backgauge arm that is required to avoid the backgauge finger colliding with the bending part. Retraction is performed once the bending part has been clamped between top and bottom tools.

Shows the distance by which the backgauge finger is retracted from the contact edge before the bend is made.

Z-retraction

Shows the extension of the side backgauge.

R position

Shows the R position of the backgauge finger against the contact edge of the bending part.

Z position

Shows the Z-position of the backgauge finger against the contact edge of the bending part.
