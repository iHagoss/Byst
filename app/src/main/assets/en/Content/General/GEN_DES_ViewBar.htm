View bars

The View bar can be used to manage the display in the 3D window. The full View bar is available in the Produce and Edit technology menus. A reduced View bar is available in the Tools menu. The View bar is divided into the following areas:

Zoom functions

In this view you can change the size of the display in the view window.

  • Zoom in
  • Zoom out
  • Center the active bending step to fit the screen size
  • Center the bending part for each bending step to fit the screen size
Position

In this area you can change the position of what is displayed in the view window. The view is moved in the opposite direction from that pointed by the arrow.

  • Move the view to the right
  • Move the view to the left
  • Move the view downwards
  • Move the view upwards

Info: The button is not visible in all the views.

 

Alignment

In this area you specify the orientation you require for the view in the display window.

  • Shows a plan view in the 3D window.
  • Shows a view from the left in the 3D window.
  • Shows a view from the right in the 3D window.
  • An isometric projection view is shown in the 3D window.
Tool display

This area controls the display of various machine components.

  • Enables/disables display of the bottom tools.
  • Enables/disables display of the top tools.
  • Enables/disables display of the machine.
Bending process display
  • Shows the current bending process before running it.
Edit

Switches the angle measuring scale off and on. While the angle measuring scale is hidden, you cannot create another bend by selecting it on the part.

All the functions in the toolbar are available to use and you can delete selected bends.

Info: The button can be found in the profile editor.

Measure
  • Specifies the length of the selected side in the view.

    • Fig.: Length of one side

    If bend lines are at right angles to each other, the program displays the length of both sides (A).

    Fig.: Length of multiple sides

  • Measures the distance between two points in three-dimensional space.
  • If the two points are located on the same axis, only one distance is shown.

    • Fig.: Length on one axis

  • If the two points are located on the same plane, three distances are shown.

    • Fig.: Length on one plane

  • If the two points are located in three dimensions, four distances are shown.

    • Fig.: Length in three dimensions
